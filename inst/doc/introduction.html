The package "dieroller" is a minimal implementation for simulating rolling a die a given number of times, and to visualize the relative frequencies in such a series of roll.

Creating a die

The first step is to create a "die" object with the function die():

fair_die <- die() 
fair_die
#> object "die"
#> 
#>   sides      prob
#> 1     1 0.1666667
#> 2     2 0.1666667
#> 3     3 0.1666667
#> 4     4 0.1666667
#> 5     5 0.1666667
#> 6     6 0.1666667

By default, die() creates a fair die

You can change the defatuls with arguments sides and prob:

loaded_die <- die(prob = c(0.075, 0.1, 0.125, 0.15, 0.20, 0.35))
loaded_die
#> object "die"
#> 
#>   sides  prob
#> 1     1 0.075
#> 2     2 0.100
#> 3     3 0.125
#> 4     4 0.150
#> 5     5 0.200
#> 6     6 0.350

rolling a die

Once you have defined a "die" object, you can use the function roll() to roll a coin a given number of times. The output will be an object of class "rool", which will contain the vector of roll:

set.seed(123)
fair_50rolls <- roll(fair_die, times = 50)
fair_50rolls
#> object "roll"
#> 
#>  [1] 3 6 4 1 1 2 5 1 5 4 1 4 6 5 2 1 3 2 3 1 1 6 5 1 5 6 5 5 3 2 1 1 6 6 2
#> [36] 4 6 3 3 3 2 4 4 4 2 2 3 4 3 1

The summary() function gives you a nice table of frequencies:

set.seed(123)
fair_50rolls <- roll(fair_die, times = 50) 
fair50_sum <- summary(fair_50rolls)
fair50_sum
#> summary "toss"
#> 
#>   side count prop
#> 1    1    11 0.22
#> 2    2     8 0.16
#> 3    3     9 0.18
#> 4    4     8 0.16
#> 5    5     7 0.14
#> 6    6     7 0.14

Plot of relative frequencies

You can use the function plot() to create a graohic with the relative frequencies of a series of die roll:

plot(fair_50rolls)
